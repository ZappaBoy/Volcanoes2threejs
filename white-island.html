White Island

Located in New Zealand

About it

Whakaari/White Island lies in northern end of the Taupo Volcanic Zone. The Taupo Volcanic Zone forms the southernmost segment of the 2,800 km. For the past few thousand years, Whakaari/White Island has been the location for an open, highly reactive hydrothermal system. This hydrothermal system expresses itself as hot springs and mud pools, fumaroles, and acid streams and lakes. The present day centre for volcanic activity and outgassing is a large crater lake of boiling acidic waters located in the western subcrater.

The crater lake of boiling acidic water, which occupies the western subcrater, appears to vary in volume due to changing meteorological conditions and fluctuating levels of hydrothermal activity. In 2012 the island showed signs of increased activity with lake and gas levels rising from inside the crater and a minor eruption occurred, sending ash into the air. More eruptions have followed since. Ongoing volcanic activity and tremors on 25 January 2013 suggested another eruption was imminent. A small eruption occurred in 2013, lasting for ten minutes and producing mostly steam.

The island is typically on an alert level rating of 1 or 2 on a scale of 0 to 5; as "Level 2 is the highest alert level before an eruption takes place.
